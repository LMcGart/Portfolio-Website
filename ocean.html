Ocean Keyboard

A unique custom mechanical keyboard kit designed to be the ideal combination of affordability, quality, and aesthetics.

May 2022 - August 2022

Project Description

The Ocean Keyboard was an extension of my business, Ocean Keys, which I started over the pandemic. Originally, I was importing mechanical keyboard parts and selling them to customers all across the United States. Over time I got more involved in the mechanical keyboard community and decided to start designing and building my own keyboard to fill a gap in the hobby that I thought needed to be filled.

The Problem

When I started this project, the custom mechanical keyboard scene was very hard to get into. It was extremely expensive with budget keyboard options starting at around $250 fully built. Parts were also hard to source and the build process was confusing to beginners. Also, the budget keyboard options were either very plasticky or used a PCB mounting system that was not optimal, creating lackluster keystroke sounds, one of the most sought after qualities from hobbyists. My end goal with the Ocean Keyboard was to make a keyboard kit that was accessible and affordable for all. As I'll talk about further below, I started out creating the Ocean Keyboard using stacked pieces of lasercut acrylic. After designing and going through a few iterations of this first prototype, I began working on a version of the keyboard that was built using an SLA 3D printer.

Proposed Solution

At the time of building, most custom keyboards utilized a clamshell design where the top and bottom cases would be tightened together using screws. Even many of the 3D printed or acrylic keyboards used screws, potentially leading to thread stripping issues that would ruin the keyboard. The Ocean Keyboard introduces a first-of-kind magnetic mounting system that connects the top and bottom cases together using magnets. This negates potential threading issues, makes parts easily replaceable, and creates an easy and approachable build process. Additionally, using an SLA 3D printe creates an affordable, yet quality, keyboard case.
